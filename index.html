Peder Schaefer is an intern for POLITICO Magazine and a freelance journalist based in Baltimore, Maryland. He has written on the transformation of rural politics in the United States and the Libertarian Party for POLITICO, reported on perjury trials for the New York Times as well as housing and business development issues for the Baltimore Banner and Baltimore Sun. He has also covered the uranium industry, the fossil fuel industry and the history of Japanese American internment (along with a heavy dose of local government reporting ) for the Buffalo Bulletin in rural Wyoming. On top of that, he has written for a number of outlets in Rhode Island. Born and raised in Providence, Rhode Island he has reported from his home state, Washington D.C., Wyoming, Baltimore and across the country and abroad for his newsletter, Rhode Trip. He's filed for publications such as the New York Times, POLITICO, Boston Globe, NBC News, Baltimore Sun, Brown Alumni Magazine, the College Hill Independent, Providence Journal, Buffalo Bulletin, Warwick Beacon, Baltimore Brew and the Harford Aegis.​​​​​​​ If you want to work on a story with Peder reach out at schaeferp18 [at] gmail [dot] com.
